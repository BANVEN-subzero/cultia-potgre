"A child who does not cry will die on its mother's back"

Origin and Meaning

This visceral and direct proverb, common throughout Cameroon, uses the powerful image of an infant carried in a traditional wrapper to teach a vital lesson about communication. It suggests that even those who care for us most deeply cannot help us if they are unaware of our suffering or our needs.

It serves as a strong encouragement to advocate for oneself. In a social context, it means that if you are facing injustice, hunger, or difficulty, you must speak up. Silence in the face of need is not a virtue; it is a danger to one's own well-being.

Cultural Context

While Cameroonian culture places high value on respect for elders and humility, this proverb balances those values by emphasizing personal responsibility. It was often used by parents to encourage shy children or by community leaders to urge citizens to participate in public discourse. It highlights that in a community, help is available, but the first step must come from the individual.

Modern Applications

In the modern world, this proverb is a powerful call for self-advocacy in healthcare, education, and the workplace. It reminds us that "suffering in silence" is often counterproductive. Whether it's mental health awareness or standing up for civil rights, the act of "crying out"—expressing one's truth—is the essential prerequisite for support and change.
