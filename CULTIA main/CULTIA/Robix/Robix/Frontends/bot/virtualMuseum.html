Welcome to the CULTIA Gallery

Grassfields

Bamileke Regalia

Discover the Toghu cloth, Nkwāl masks, and royal stools that represent the intricate social hierarchy and artistic mastery of the Western Highlands.

Sultanate

Bamum Chronicles

The Shumom script, palace archives, and legendary bronze pipes of the Bamum people, showcasing one of Africa's few indigenous writing systems.

Northwest

Nso Sacred Arts

Explore the Ntieya cloth and Kwifon staffs—symbols of the powerful secret societies that balance governance in the Nso Kingdom.

Coastal Forest

Bassa Soul

The Mvet harp and Nganga drums—the rhythmic heartbeat of the Bassa people used to transmit oral history and ancestral wisdom.

Sahel

Fulani Elegance

Intricate silver jewelry, leatherwork, and traditional nomadic gear that define the identity of the Sahelian pastoralists.

Sawa

Duala Maritime

Ceremonial pirogues and Ngondo festival artifacts that celebrate the deep spiritual connection between the Sawa people and the sea.

Northwest

Bafut Palace Treasures

Sacred carvings and architectural wonders from the Bafut Palace, one of the oldest and best-preserved royal residences in the Grassfields.

Southwest

Bakweri Heritage

Artifacts related to the 'Efasa-Moto' mountain god and the traditional agricultural tools used on the fertile slopes of Mount Cameroon.

Manengouba

Bakossi Forest Arts

Sacred objects from the Manengouba forest, representing the spirits of the Twin Lakes and the ancestral wisdom of the Bakossi people.

Forest Zone

Beti Ancestral Masks

Intricate wooden masks and statues used in the 'So' initiation rites, reflecting the spiritual depth of the forest-dwelling Beti-Pahuin groups.

Far North

Kotoko Bronzes

Ancient bronze riders and tiny talismans from the Sao civilization, the ancestors of the Kotoko people living along the Logone River.

Mandara Mtns

Mafa Mandara Arts

Iron tools and unique pottery from the Mandara Mountains, showcasing the Mafa people's mastery of terrace farming and metallurgy.
