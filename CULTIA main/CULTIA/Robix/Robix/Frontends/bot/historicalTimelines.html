Chronicles of Cameroon

Journey through the pivotal moments that shaped the destiny of "Africa in Miniature".

1000 BCE

Iron Age Foundations

Archaeological evidence in southern Cameroon reveals the early start of the Iron Age, marking the beginning of sophisticated tool-making and social structures.

15th Century

The Rio dos Camarões

Portuguese explorer Fernão do Pó arrives at the Wouri River. Impressed by the abundance of ghost shrimps, he names it "Rio dos Camarões", the origin of the name Cameroon.

1884

German Protectorate

The Treaty of Germano-Duala is signed, establishing Kamerun as a German protectorate. This period saw major infrastructural developments and the drawing of modern borders.

1918 - 1960

Mandate & Trusteeship

Following WWI, Cameroon is divided between France and Britain. This dual colonial heritage profoundly influenced the nation's legal, educational, and linguistic systems.

1960

Sovereign Independence

French Cameroon achieves independence on January 1st. Ahmadou Ahidjo becomes the first president, steering the new nation through its foundational years.

1961

The Foumban Unification

Following a plebiscite, the British Southern Cameroons joins the Republic of Cameroon to form the Federal Republic of Cameroon, a unique bilingual union.

1982

Leadership Transition

President Ahidjo resigns and is succeeded by Paul Biya. This marked the beginning of a new political era focused on "Renewal" and "rigor and moralization".
