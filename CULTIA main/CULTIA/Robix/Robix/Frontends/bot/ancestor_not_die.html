The Ancestor Who Would Not Die

The Ancestor Who Would Not Die

One Bassa folktale tells of an old man named Ndo'o, who refused to die because his children were constantly fighting

The Long-Lived Elder

In a village of the Bassa people, there lived an old man named Ndo'o who had reached the remarkable age of 120 years. Despite his advanced years, he remained strong and healthy, with a sharp mind and a kind heart. The villagers often wondered at his longevity and consulted him for wisdom on important matters.

Ndo'o had raised seven children, each of whom had grown to adulthood and started their own families. However, as the years passed, his children began to quarrel with one another over inheritance, land boundaries, and family responsibilities. The arguments grew louder and more frequent, causing distress throughout the village.

The Children's Disrespect

When Ndo'o grew too old to work in the fields, his children began to treat him with less respect. They would argue about who should care for him, with each sibling trying to avoid the responsibility. Some would leave him with barely enough food, while others would take his belongings without asking.

The old man's heart was broken by his children's behavior. He had always taught them the importance of family unity and respect for elders, but now they seemed to have forgotten these values. Despite his sadness, Ndo'o continued to offer guidance and wisdom whenever his children would listen.

The Refusal to Die

As Ndo'o's children continued to fight among themselves, the old man made a decision that would astonish the village. He called his family together and announced, "I will not die until you learn to live in peace." The villagers were amazed by his declaration, but his children simply rolled their eyes, thinking it was just the rambling of an old man.

From that day forward, Ndo'o seemed to grow stronger rather than weaker. While his children continued their quarrels, he remained healthy and active, tending his small garden and helping neighbors in need. The more his children fought, the more determined he became to stay alive.

The Village's Concern

The other villagers began to worry about the situation. They respected Ndo'o but were concerned about his family's well-being. The constant arguments disrupted the peace of the entire village, and people began to avoid the family's compound.

The village elders called a meeting to discuss what could be done. They approached Ndo'o to ask if there was anything they could do to help resolve the family conflict. The old man simply replied, "My children must find their own way to peace. I cannot force them to love one another."

The Turning Point

One day, a terrible accident occurred. The youngest of Ndo'o's grandchildren fell from a tree and broke his leg. In their panic, all of the siblings came together to care for the child, putting aside their differences to ensure he received proper treatment. They worked as a team, with each contributing their skills and resources.

As they cared for the injured child, the siblings began to remember why they had once been close. They shared stories of their childhood, laughed at old memories, and realized how much they had lost by allowing greed and pride to divide them.

Reconciliation and Release

When the child had recovered, the siblings approached their father with tears of remorse. They apologized for their behavior and promised to treat him with the respect he deserved. They also pledged to work together as a family, sharing responsibilities and supporting one another.

Ndo'o was deeply moved by his children's change of heart. He embraced each of them and blessed their renewed commitment to family unity. That night, surrounded by his reconciled family, the old man passed away peacefully in his sleep.

The story of Ndo'o became a powerful lesson in the Bassa community about the importance of family harmony and respect for elders. It reminded people that true wealth lies not in material possessions but in the love and unity of family. The tale also emphasized that wisdom and guidance from ancestors should be treasured, not taken for granted.
